About Us

Our story

Ghanaian Aduane was founded in 2026 by Chef Cierra Blackett, a chef who grew up learning to cook beside her Accra-born mother. When growing up in New York City, Cierra noticed something was missing — the bold flavors of home.

What started as a small pop-up at community events quickly grew into a beloved Harlem restaurant. Today, Ghanaian Aduane seats 80 guests in a warm space decorated with kente cloth and hand-carved fixtures imported from Accra.

Our mission is simple: feed people the way a Ghanaian mother would. Every dish is made from scratch, every broth simmered low and slow, every plate served with pride. Whether you're Ghanaian and homesick, or a curious New Yorker — you belong at our table.
